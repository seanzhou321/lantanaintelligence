The Methodology

Why I Started This

The AI coding era creates a specific problem that most teams are not equipped to see: AI generation makes structural decisions silently, at a speed that outpaces every governance model that depends on periodic review. Specification quality becomes the binding constraint that determines whether AI multiplies velocity or chaos.

I spent two years researching this problem systematically across three dimensions — development methodology, architecture governance, and verification — producing the three papers below. The consulting practice is the applied extension of that research: working directly with teams to close the gap between where their process is and where it needs to be.

The papers are available for review under a non-distribution agreement prior to publication. If you are an engineering leader evaluating this work, contact me.

The SBR Framework Papers

Three connected papers currently under peer review. Together they form the intellectual foundation of the consulting practice.

Waterfall and Agile in the Era of AI Coding

AI shifts the binding constraint from implementation to specification. Neither Waterfall nor Agile ports cleanly into the AI era. The paper identifies the hybrid pattern best-performing teams are converging on: Agile cadence, Waterfall-quality specification, AI implementation.

Architecture Governance in the Era of AI Coding

AI makes structural decisions less visible, not less important. The paper proposes a continuous, specification-anchored governance model covering both regulated/safety-critical systems and commercial software with evolving requirements.

TDD, BDD, and the Stratified Behavioral Refinement Framework

Introduces the SBR framework and its central concept: the trust boundary — the provability boundary of the test suite, defining the region within which autonomous AI generation is verifiable. The framework integrates EARS requirements, spec-driven methodology, architecture governance, TDD, and BDD into a directed chain for AI-assisted development.

How I Work

The principles that guide every engagement.

Integrity

Recommendations are evidence-based. If the SBR framework does not address a team's problem, I will say so. Clients deserve honest assessment, not a methodology sale.

Rigor

The SBR framework is grounded in formal verification theory, empirical research, and practitioner evidence. The same intellectual discipline applies to every engagement.

Partnership

I work directly alongside the engineers and engineering leaders I engage with — not as a detached advisor delivering slide decks, but as a practitioner embedded in the actual work.

Impact

Success is measured by what changes in how a team works — not by the number of sessions delivered. Every engagement is accountable to outcomes the team can see and verify.

Scope Honesty

This is a one-person practice with a defined methodology focus. I do not offer services I cannot deliver at depth. What I offer, I deliver personally and with full commitment.

Responsibility

AI-assisted development carries real consequences for software quality and team culture. The SBR framework keeps humans accountable for specification and design — by structure, not by hope.
